01

Vision & Mission

Give every hospitality owner sight.

Vision

A world where no good café closes because of leaks it never saw.

Most hospitality businesses don't fail from lack of effort or talent. They fail from a slow loss of margin nobody was watching. We want to make that loss visible and fixable for every operator, not just the ones who can afford a finance team.

Mission

Every Monday, know exactly where your profit leaked.

We read the files an owner already has, every week, and tell them plainly where the money is going and what to do about it. Done for them, human-reviewed, and honest about what it can and cannot prove.

What we believe about the product

  • The audit is the front door. We earn trust by doing the hard analysis once, brilliantly, before we ask anyone to commit to anything ongoing.
  • Your own history is the benchmark. The most useful comparison is not an industry average; it is your own last quarter. Drift only shows up against a baseline.
  • Weekly beats quarterly. Costs now move weekly. Hindsight at BAS time is too late to steer.
  • Refusing to guess is a feature. Where a number can't be verified, we say so. Trust is built by what a tool declines to claim.
02

Product philosophy

Done-for-you, evidence-first, human-reviewed.

Leak is not software you have to learn. It is a piece of work delivered to you, powered by an analysis engine and finished by a person who has run cafés. Five principles govern how we build.

Work delivered, not a tool to operate

The owner's job is to send files and read a plan. The effort is ours. Every design decision reduces what we ask of them.

Measured before advisory

Findings from a customer's own files, with stated conservative fractions, rank above general advice. We separate the two and never blur them.

One clear action beats forty

We rank leaks by annual dollar impact and lead with the biggest. A plan you can start on Monday beats a report you never finish.

Honesty compounds

Conservative numbers, visible working, and honest caveats build the trust that turns an audit into a subscription and a referral.

The engine serves the operator

Software finds patterns; a person who has stood behind the machine decides what they mean. That order never reverses.

Give value before the ask

Free tools and honest education come first. The paid audit is what that earned trust converts into.

03

Problems we solve · who we solve them for

Margin that leaks invisibly, for people with no time to look.

The problems

  • Invisible cost drift. Supplier prices creep, rosters stop matching trade, menu items go underwater. None of it announces itself.
  • Wrong resolution. Owners watch sales daily and costs monthly. The gap between those habits is where margin disappears.
  • No time and no analyst. A single café can't justify a full-time finance person, yet lives and dies on a few points of margin.
  • A worsening squeeze. Payday super, a 4.75% award wage rise, and the October card-surcharge ban all landed in 2026, tightening margins further.
  • Tools that show takings, not truth. The POS shows what came in. Almost nothing an owner has shows what was kept, or why.

Ideal customer profiles

ICP 1 · Core

Independent café owner-operator

One to three sites, AU. Owner works in the business, is margin-aware but time-poor, and has the files but not the hours. The audit's home.

ICP 2 · Expansion

Small multi-site group

Three to ten venues. Feels the same leaks at scale, wants comparison across locations. The subscription and multi-location tier's future home.

ICP 3 · Amplifier

Accountants & bookkeepers

Not the buyer, the referrer. Leak does the operational read they don't, so they send clients. A compounding acquisition channel.

Personas

"Sam", the owner-operator

Ran a strong week, opened the bank on Monday, and it didn't add up. Proud, allergic to being sold to, sharp about their trade but unsure of their real cost of goods. Wants a straight answer from someone who has done the job.

"Priya", the multi-site manager

Runs four venues, can feel that one is leaking but can't prove which or where. Needs comparison, ranked actions, and something her owners will actually read.

AI · Privacy · Data · Security

The promises under the product.

AI philosophy

The machine flags. A person decides.

Our engine is deterministic analysis validated against ground truth, not a chatbot guessing at numbers. Language models are a fallback seam for reading messy documents, off by default, and never the source of truth for a figure. Where a number can't be verified from the files, we label it, we don't estimate it. Every finding is human-reviewed before a customer sees it. AI earns its place only by making the honest answer faster, never by inventing a confident one.

Privacy philosophy

Your data stays yours.

Files are used for your audit and nothing else. No resale, no "insights sharing," no training a public model on your books. The free tools run in the browser and keep your numbers on your device. You can request deletion at any time, and it is honoured fully.

Data philosophy

Less, but true, beats plenty but vague.

We collect only the files the audit needs: sales, wages, invoices. Your own history is the primary benchmark; external benchmarks are official and cited. Uncertainty is surfaced, not smoothed over. A conservative number we can defend is worth more than a bold one we can't.

Security philosophy

The boring kind, proven not assumed.

Every business is isolated at the database with Postgres row-level security, and that isolation is verified by a suite of cross-tenant attack tests, not taken on faith. Admin access is an explicit allowlist. Destructive actions are guarded. We make no claim we can't stand behind, so no "military-grade" language, just strict isolation and human review.

07

Technical architecture · high level

How it's built.

  • Application: Next.js 14 (App Router), server actions, deployed on Vercel.
  • Data & auth: Supabase (Postgres) with row-level security as the isolation source of truth; migration-driven schema.
  • Billing: Stripe via a lean REST client and a pure, tested signature-verification core.
  • Analysis: a pure JavaScript engine (lib/engine) plus a costing library, both framework-independent and unit-tested against ground truth.
  • Ingestion: serverless PDF text extraction, a heuristic invoice parser, and email-in routing by per-business token.
  • Extensibility seams: OCR and LLM fallbacks exist behind flags, off by default, no-op cleanly. New detectors register onto the engine without touching its maths.
  • Testing: engine ground-truth, invoice fixtures, Stripe core, dozens of RLS attack tests, and query-plan checks, all in one suite.
  • Principle: the maths is separable and proven; the framework around it can change without risking the numbers.

Deliberate constraint: pure, testable analysis at the core means the engine could be reused across surfaces (app, public tools, future integrations) without re-validating the maths each time.

Customer journey · onboarding · retention

From a scroll to a habit.

The journey

  • Discover: a Reel, a benchmark post, or a free tool. Value first, no ask.
  • Engage: run the free COGS or menu check. A real number, a small surprise, an email captured.
  • Book: the enquiry form, at the founding price, with the guarantee removing the risk.
  • Send: three months of files by upload or email. No setup. The only real effort, and it's an hour.
  • Receive: the engine reads, a human reviews, the Profit Improvement Plan arrives: ranked, sized, actionable.
  • Act & verify: work the roadmap, watch leaks move to "banked" in the savings ledger as later weeks prove them.
  • Continue: the weekly monitoring subscription keeps the sight on, so leaks are caught while they're still small.

Onboarding Shipped

"Send the files you already have." An in-app onboarding flow plus email-in means no integration, no data entry, no software to learn. The first win (the plan) arrives without the customer configuring anything.

Retention Shipped

The Monday briefing delivers the promise weekly. The savings ledger's "since you started" band proves the subscription pays for itself. Reminders sustain the habit. Retention is engineered as visible, compounding value, not lock-in.

Earn the trust once, then keep it.

$499 for the first 5 founding clients, then $999 standard. One fixed fee, no subscription required to buy it. Guarantee: identify at least twice the fee in annual opportunities, or you don't pay.

The founding rate is real scarcity, five clients, then it ends. It is never dressed up with a fake countdown.

Ongoing In-app, not yet sold on site

Weekly monitoring subscription

The private follow-on: weekly reports, the savings ledger, drift alerts. Billing is fully built in Stripe. Historically modelled at Starter $99 / Pro $199 (Pro adds trends and multi-location); public subscription pricing is under review post-repositioning and is deliberately not sold on the marketing site yet.

Future pricing directions Idea

  • Audit-to-subscription funnel as the core motion: the audit proves value, the subscription keeps it.
  • Value-indexed tiers that scale with sites or with verified savings, so price tracks the profit recovered.
  • Partner / accountant pricing for referral volume, once the amplifier channel is proven.

Business-model figures (conversion, churn, blended subscription value, year-one projections) live in the accountant review deck and are explicitly modelled assumptions, not observed results. They are refined as real cohorts arrive.

Success metrics · North Star

One number that means we're working.

North Star Metric

Verified dollars of profit recovered for customers.

Not revenue, not sign-ups, not reports sent. The savings ledger already computes this honestly: a leak counts as recovered only when a later clean week proves it, and it reverses if it relapses. It is the one metric that is impossible to grow without genuinely helping an owner, which is exactly why it should govern the roadmap. Grow the profit we provably recover, and revenue, retention and referrals follow.

Supporting metrics

Acquisition

Free-tool completions, enquiry rate, audits booked, cost per booked audit.

Value delivered

Opportunity identified per audit (guarantee coverage), findings acted on, time-to-first-action.

Retention

Audit-to-subscription conversion, subscription churn, recovered run-rate held over time.

Trust & growth

Referral rate (especially accountant-sourced), review sentiment, share and save rates on content.
