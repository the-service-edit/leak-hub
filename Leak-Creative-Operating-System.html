01

Creative Vision

The feeling comes first.

Design is downstream of a feeling. Before a single frame is composed, we agree on the emotion it has to create. Every Leak post is engineered to leave one impression: someone competent finally understands my business, and they are calm about it.

How an owner should feel

  • Steadier, not scared. We never sell with anxiety. The feeling is relief, the way a good accountant telling you the truth kindly makes you feel, not the panic of a countdown timer.
  • Respected. The reader is an intelligent operator who is time-poor, not a beginner. We assume competence and add clarity.
  • Seen. "That is exactly my Monday." Recognition is our strongest emotion. It earns the save and the quiet screenshot to a business partner.
  • In good hands. Premium, precise, unhurried. The design should feel like the inside of a very well-run café: nothing extra, everything considered.

How owners should describe us

If you overheard an owner describe Leak to another owner, these are the words we are designing for, and the words we are designing against.

02

Design Language

One system, no exceptions.

Every element below is drawn from the live product tokens, so a social graphic, the app and a sales deck are visibly the same company. Nothing here is decorative. Each choice earns its place by making the message clearer.

Colour

Paper dominates. Purple is structure, not decoration. The burnt-orange leak is punctuation, never a fill. A rough balance to hold in your eye: about 70% paper and neutrals, 25% purple family, 5% ink, and a trace of leak-orange.

Extended family for gradients and dark surfaces: violet #7C3AED, violet-light #8B5CF6, purple-deep #4C1D95, lavender-soft #F5F3FF, charcoal #1C2128. Three approved gradients only: the deep radial purple, the soft lavender-to-paper wash, and the near-black premium gradient. No other gradients are ever introduced.

Typography

One typeface, everywhere: Plus Jakarta Sans. No serif, ever, including the word "Leak". Type leads every layout; it is the design. Weights carry meaning: 800 for display and numbers, 700 for kickers and CTAs, 500 to 600 for body. Kickers are uppercase with 0.09em to 0.14em tracking in grey; headlines are tight (-0.02em) and confident.

Number 800$7,300
Headline 800Where your profit leaked
Kicker 700Profit leak breakdown
Body 500Plain English, sized annually, with one thing to do about it.

Canvas type scale (px on a 1080 artboard): 22, 26, 30, 36, 44, 54, 66, 80, 96, 120, 150. Pick from the scale; never invent an in-between size.

Spacing & grid

  • 8-point rhythm. All spacing steps in multiples of 8. Generosity over density, always.
  • Artboard-relative. Everything scales from the canvas width, so one layout holds at every format from a story to a billboard.
  • Safe margins are law. Key copy never enters the platform UI zone or the outer safe band. Stories keep clear of the top and bottom chrome.
  • Left-aligned editorial. The default is a strong left edge, like a well-set page. Centre only for single statements and quotes.

Negative space & hierarchy

  • Whitespace is a feature, not a gap. Aim to leave roughly 40% of any frame empty. Air signals confidence and premium.
  • One hero per frame. A single element wins: the headline, or the number, or the photo. Everything else supports it and is visibly quieter.
  • Read in one path. Kicker, then hero, then support, then the lockup. A viewer's eye should never have to choose where to start.

Cards, shapes & depth

Surfaces float on paper with soft, warm, layered shadows. We build depth from light, not from lines. Radii are fixed: 14px for small chips, 20px for cards, 26px for hero surfaces. Shapes are limited to rounded rectangles, pills, the accent rule, the ring and the dot. No hard 1px boxes in premium contexts, no drop-shadows that read as harsh, no strokes used decoratively.

shadow-card

The resting state. 0 1px 2px / 0 6px 20px at 4 to 5% ink. Quiet lift off the paper.

shadow-lift

Hover and hero state. 0 4px 12px / 0 18px 46px at 6 to 9%. Never heavier than this.

Panel fill

Lavender-soft on light, or 10 to 14% white on dark. Panels group; they never cage.

The six surfaces

Every graphic is built on one of exactly six visual modes. They are moods, not new brands: same type, same dot, same rules. Choosing a mode is choosing a temperature, not reinventing the look.

A tiny, closed set of marks. These are the only decorative shapes Leak owns. Everything else is type, photo or data. We reach for a motif to clarify, never to fill space.

Image treatment, illustration & texture

  • Photography is full-bleed. Real photos fill the frame, corners rounded to 20 to 28px, with a bottom scrim so type stays legible over any image. See Part 04.
  • We do not illustrate. No mascots, no spot illustrations, no stock vectors. If a concept needs explaining, we use a diagram built from our own shapes, or a photograph.
  • No texture. The only texture is paper. No noise, no grain overlays, no patterns, no gradients beyond the three approved.
  • Data is a first-class citizen. Charts use the purple family on paper, tabular numerals, generous spacing, and a visible source line. A chart should be as beautiful as a headline.
  • Composition is editorial. Strong left edge, big type, deliberate air. Centre-weight only statements, quotes and single numbers.
  • The dot is sacred. It is never removed, recoloured off-palette, animated gratuitously, or replaced with an emoji.

Social Design System

Templates, not one-offs.

Nothing is designed from scratch. Every post is an instance of a template with a fixed anatomy, so a new post is a decision about content, never about layout. The shared anatomy is always the same four zones.

Kicker · what this is
Hero · the one idea (type, number or photo)
Support · one line, optional
Lockup · mark + leak.cafe

The universal anatomy

  • Kicker (top): a small uppercase label naming the post type. Orientation in one glance.
  • Hero (centre of gravity): the single message. This is 60 to 70% of the frame's attention.
  • Support (optional): one quiet line that adds the lesson or the source.
  • Lockup (bottom): the ring, the wordmark and leak.cafe. Always present, always the same position.

Every template below is this anatomy, tuned. They map directly to the modes and families already in the Studio, so "designed" and "buildable" are the same thing.

04

Photography Direction

Documentary, never staged.

Leak's photography looks like a real café on a real morning, because our credibility is that we have actually stood behind the machine. Styled, stock-looking imagery quietly says the opposite. The house look: warm, natural, a little imperfect, always legible.

The house look

  • Lighting: natural only. Light from the side or behind, soft and directional, ideally early morning. Flash is banned. Embrace a slightly moody, low-key frame over a flat, evenly-lit one.
  • Colour grade: warm and true. Whites lean paper-warm, blacks lifted a touch, saturation held back. No teal-and-orange, no heavy filters, no fashion contrast. One consistent grade across every image.
  • Crop: vertical 4:5 for feed, 9:16 for stories. Compose with the lower third kept calm so type and scrim sit cleanly.
  • Depth of field: shallow for people and product (subject sharp, café soft), deeper for rooms so the space reads. Roughly f2 to f2.8 close, f4 to f5.6 wide.

By subject

  • Hospitality & rooms: from behind the machine or over the pass. Steam, hands, movement. Blur is a feature, not a mistake.
  • Product & food: top-down on a working bench, real portions, honest plating. The trim and the waste are allowed in frame; that is the point.
  • People & founder: eye-level, chest to waist up, café soft behind. Warm and direct, never a corporate headshot. Mel looks at the lens, not past it.
  • Workspace & documents: invoices, rosters, the report on a laptop. Legibility beats beauty; crop or blur anything sensitive.
  • Behind the scenes: the quiet before open, closing up alone, the delivery at the back door. The unglamorous truth of the trade.

Editing rules

One grade, applied consistently. Straighten and lightly crop, correct exposure and warmth, and stop. No skin smoothing, no sky replacement, no removing the mess that makes it real. If an image needs heavy retouching to work, it was the wrong frame. A slightly imperfect true photo always beats a perfect fake one.

05

Motion Direction

Calm, editorial, considered.

Leak does not animate to entertain. Motion exists to help the eye understand order and to give one small, earned moment of delight. Content settles into place, as if it were always there. The feeling is a premium product easing open, never a banner demanding attention.

Principles

  • Settle, do not bounce. Elements rise and fade into position on the house curve. The only permitted overshoot is a single number popping, once.
  • One moment of delight. Per graphic, one thing may animate for feeling: the hero number counting up, or the health ring drawing. Never two.
  • Transform and opacity only. We animate position and fade, nothing else. Layout space is reserved from the first frame, so there is zero layout shift, ever.
  • Respect the viewer. Reduced-motion is honoured everywhere. When it is on, everything is simply present, instantly and correctly.
  • Never decorative. No loops, no parallax, no spinning marks, no kinetic type for its own sake.

The spec

  • House easing: cubic-bezier(0.21, 0.9, 0.35, 1). Confident, decelerating, calm.
  • Entrance (rise + fade): 450ms, up 8 to 12px.
  • Micro-interactions: 150ms. Hover lifts a card by 1 to 2px into shadow-lift.
  • The one delight: count-up 1.3s, ring draw 1.4s, on cubic-bezier(0.22, 0.61, 0.36, 1).
  • Overshoot (rare): a number may use cubic-bezier(0.34, 1.56, 0.64, 1), once.
  • Stagger: 60 to 90ms between sequential items. Never more.

These are the live values from the product. A Reel, an app screen and a keynote build should all move with the same temperament.

06

Creative Principles

Ten laws. No exceptions.

When a decision is unclear, these settle it. They are deliberately strict, because consistency is built by what we refuse to do. If a principle and a clever idea disagree, the principle wins.

01

One message per graphic.

If it says two things, it says nothing. Split it.

02

Whitespace is valuable.

Air is not wasted space. It is the signal of a premium, confident brand. Leave the 40%.

03

Typography leads.

The words are the design. Set them beautifully and most graphics need nothing else.

04

Data should be beautiful, and honest.

Numbers are heroes: large, tabular, sourced. We never dress up a figure we cannot stand behind.

05

Photography tells the truth.

Real over styled, always. A true imperfect frame beats a perfect fake one.

06

Depth from light, not lines.

Soft shadows on paper. No hard boxes, no harsh strokes, no cages.

07

One warm accent, used like punctuation.

The leak-orange is a trace, never a fill. Restraint is the brand.

08

The dot is sacred.

Always present, always brand purple, never an emoji, never redrawn.

09

Never manufacture urgency.

No countdowns of anxiety, no false scarcity. Our only urgency is real: a date, or five founding spots.

10

When in doubt, remove.

Never decorate. Always simplify. The best Leak design is the one with nothing left to take away.

07

Brand Guardian

The pre-flight checklist.

Nothing publishes until it passes every line. If a piece fails one, it does not go out with an apology; it goes back. The "fails if" column is the fast way to catch the usual slips.

Brand consistency
Uses one of the six modes, Plus Jakarta Sans, the palette and the lockup, unchanged.
Fails if: a new colour, a second typeface, or a redrawn mark appears.
Hierarchy
One clear hero. The eye knows exactly where to start and where to go next.
Fails if: two elements compete, or the reader has to hunt for the point.
Typography
Sizes drawn from the scale, tight display tracking, kicker in grey uppercase. No serif anywhere.
Fails if: an off-scale size, a stray font, or centre-set body copy slips in.
Colour
Paper-dominant, purple as structure, leak-orange only as a trace.
Fails if: orange is used as a fill, or the frame is stark white with no paper warmth.
Spacing
8-point rhythm, safe margins respected, roughly 40% of the frame left as air.
Fails if: copy touches the edges, crowds the UI zone, or the frame feels dense.
Messaging
One message, in plain operator English, true to "know where your profit leaked".
Fails if: it says two things, uses jargon, or drifts into hype.
Trust
Every figure is sourced or clearly illustrative. The guarantee is stated correctly. No fake urgency.
Fails if: an unverified number, a client-result claim, or a manufactured deadline appears.
Education
The reader leaves with something useful, even if they never buy.
Fails if: it only sells, and teaches nothing.
Originality
It could only be Leak. Cover the logo and it is still ours.
Fails if: it looks like a generic template, a trend copy, or any other brand.
Professionalism
Considered, calm, premium. No emojis in the graphic, no em dashes, no clutter.
Fails if: it feels rushed, cute, cluttered or loud.

One question closes the review: if this ran on a billboard beside Stripe, Notion and Linear, would it hold its own? If not, it is not finished.

08

Build For Scale

The same brand, ten years out.

This system is defined by constraints, not by a folder of assets, which is exactly what lets it scale. Anyone, on any surface, produces on-brand Leak work by holding to the same few things: Plus Jakarta Sans, purple on paper, the ring and the dot, one idea, big type, generous air, motion that settles. Below is how the language extends as Leak grows.

Channel

Podcast

Cover art in soft-gradient with the mark and one title line. Audiograms use the drawing waveform in purple on paper; episode titles set like our statement template.

Channel

YouTube

A thumbnail system: one face or one number, four to six words of ink type, the dot bottom-right. Lower-thirds and end cards reuse the lockup and the house motion.

Channel

Events & conferences

Banners and booths are mostly paper and air with one huge line. Name badges, lanyards and signage share the kicker-and-lockup system. Restraint reads as premium at scale.

Channel

Sales presentations

A deck master: one point per slide, the six modes as slide themes, the metric-hero for numbers. A deck should feel like the feed, stretched to 16:9.

Channel

Ads

Static ads are the statement or statistic templates with a single CTA. Video ads open on the hook in two seconds and move with house motion. Never louder than the organic feed, just tighter.

Channel

Billboards

The purest test of the system: one line, or one number, and the dot. If it needs more than that to work, it is not a Leak billboard.

Channel

Investor decks

Data-led mode throughout. Numbers as heroes, always sourced, charts in the purple family. Confidence through clarity, not through gloss.

Channel

Product launches

The announcement and feature-launch templates, scaled across email, in-app, social and a landing hero, all sharing one line and one mark.

Channel

The through-line

Every surface answers one question the same way: does it feel calm, clear, premium and unmistakably Leak? If yes, it belongs. If no, it does not ship.

The ten-year rule

Protect consistency above all else.

Trends will change. The mark, the typeface, the palette and these principles do not. When a new surface, format or platform appears, we do not invent a new look for it. We ask what the Leak version is, and we build that. The goal is simple and permanent: a decade from now, a stranger should see a single frame and know, before they read a word, that it is Leak.
